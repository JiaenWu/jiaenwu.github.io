## Research

<div class="research-grid">

<!-- ===================== BALANCE ===================== -->
<div class="research-card">

<div class="research-body">
<h4 class="research-title">Human Balance and Stability</h4>

<p class="research-desc">
Understanding how humans maintain balance during walking and respond to perturbations.
Our work develops quantitative metrics of balance capability and investigates how sensory,
neuromuscular, and biomechanical factors contribute to locomotor stability.
</p>

<div class="research-body">
<h4 class="research-title">Wearable Robotics and Exoskeletons</h4>

<p class="research-desc">
Development of wearable robotic systems that assist human locomotion and improve mobility.
We study real-time control strategies, adaptive assistance, and human responses to robotic intervention during walking.
</p>

<!-- ===================== GAIT ===================== -->
<div class="research-card">
<img src="{{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/images/research/research3.png"
     class="research-thumb"
     alt="Gait analysis">

<div class="research-body">
<h4 class="research-title">Locomotion Biomechanics</h4>

<p class="research-desc">
Investigating the biomechanics and neural control of human walking using motion capture,
wearable sensors, force measurements, and perturbation experiments.
Our goal is to better understand movement variability, adaptation, and coordination.
</p>
</div>
</div>


<!-- ===================== CONTROL ===================== -->
<div class="research-card">
<img src="{{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/images/research/research4.png"
     class="research-thumb"
     alt="Control systems">

<div class="research-body">
<h4 class="research-title">Real-Time Human-in-the-Loop Control</h4>

<p class="research-desc">
Designing closed-loop control frameworks for wearable robots using real-time sensing,
gait phase estimation, and adaptive optimization.
We develop systems capable of interacting safely and robustly with humans in dynamic environments.
</p>
</div>
</div>

<p class="research-desc">
