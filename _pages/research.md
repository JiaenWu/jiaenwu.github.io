## Research Directions
Our research focuses on understanding human locomotion and balance, and developing wearable robotic systems that enhance mobility, stability, and physical performance through biomechanics, robotics, and optimal control.

<!-- Our research aims to establish a new paradigm for engineering
<strong>human balance intelligence</strong>.

Human balance underlies nearly every movement, yet remains one of the least quantified and assisted aspects of mobility.
We develop intelligent systems that perceive human motion and environment, model human–device interactions, and deliver adaptive real-time assistance through robotics, biomechanics, AI, and wearable technologies.

Our long-term goal is to create closed-loop intelligent balance systems that continuously understand, predict, and enhance human mobility in real-world environments. -->

<div class="research-topics">

Current research topics include:

<ul>
<li>Wearable sensing and embedded AI for human movement analysis</li>
<li>Human balance quantification and perturbation-response analysis</li>
<li>Foundation digital twins for human–robot interaction</li>
<li>Human-in-the-loop optimization and adaptive control</li>
<li>Real-time edge intelligence for wearable robotics</li>
<li>Neurological gait rehabilitation and assistive technologies</li>
<li>Data-driven biomechanics and locomotion modeling</li>
<li>Personalized balance assistance and rehabilitation systems</li>
</ul>

</div>
